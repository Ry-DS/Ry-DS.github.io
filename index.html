About me

The best way to predict the future is to create it

Born in Oklahoma, raised in Colombo and living in Melbourne, I am a young developer/photographer who loves to capture the art of nature by day, and code the next big thing at 2 o'clock in the morning.

I believe every person has a form of creativity within themselves, the trick is to just find out how they can express it. Ever since the age of 13, I've found my outlet to be the logistical thinking of code, and the art of photography. Throughout my 4 years (and growing!) of experience, I've had my fair share of failure, agony and frustration. Maybe a missed opportunity or a failed ambition just to name a few. But through pure passion and the love of my craft, I've used these experiences to grow as a person. I have absolutely no clue where I'll be in another 5 or 10 years, but I'm excited.

The future is in the making,
are you ready?

    50 Raw

    50 Raw is an e-learning startup, aiming to revolutionise how high school students in Victoria learn online, bringing beautiful, modern technology into the industry. It was founded and developed by 3 high-school students, including myself.

    We built 50Raw because we believed there is still so much potential for technology to have in the education sector. Unsatisfied by the current solutions we were given in our high-school years, we wanted to create a new platform which leveraged the abilities of modern technology - such as interactive animations and AI - to deliver a personalised learning experience to our students, helping them develop habits of lifelong learning.

    While we currently accept students into our platform, we are still under rapid development, and hope to integrate ourselves into the classroom, and help more students reach their full potential.

    MLH Fellowship - WFP's PRISM

    The MLH Fellowship is a 3 month long program designed to help aspiring software engineers get their career started by contributing to large open-source projects such as React and Jest.

    Out of over 20,000 applicants, I was selected to be part of the very first class, consisting of 150 students.

    Thanks to the Fellowship, I managed to grow my software engineering skills at a rapid pace, learn from some incredibly skilled individuals, and make significant open source contributions to The World Food Programme's PRISM, a mapping & analysis tool used by decision makers to better decide where to focus their humanitarian efforts.

    Workplace Utilisation Analysis Tool

    For my final development project in high-school, we were required to orchestrate a piece of software for a business within a few months. Knowing the potential this opportunity provided, I decided to aim big, and put myself into the deep-end and develop a complex 'monitor' tracking software for the reputable company, BGIS, allowing managers to understand how their differing types of monitors are being utilised within their workplace.

    Despite these complexities which forced me to learn entirely new technologies within the span of weeks, I managed to pull it off, developing WUAT, a panel and complimentary employee client suite which tracks and reports on the monitor usage of employees within different floors and spaces of the workplace.

    I am incredibly grateful for the members of BGIS who were willing to take me in and give me a chance to prove myself, despite still being in high-school. I've learnt an incredible amount about development for larger organisations which includes security concerns, agile software development and so much more. I hope WUAT continues to serve them for more years to come.

    VCE Exam Calendar Importer

    Over one weekend, being a VCE student, I was already making preparations for my finals by entering my exam times into my calendar. After about 3 exams in, I got bored, and wondered how cool it would be if an application existed that did this laborious task for you. So, instead of spending 10 minutes entering my exams into my calendar, I spent 10 hours creating this application.
    Inefficient? Yes
    Fun? Definitely.
    The project put my web scraping skills to the test, forcing me to find a way to scrape all the exam data from the official VCAA site to a file which I could use to create calendar files with. The website was received well by my peers and saved a few minutes from their day. Hopefully all those minutes collectively add up to 10 hours.
